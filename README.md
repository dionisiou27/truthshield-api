# TruthShield API

**Cognitive Security Infrastructure for Democratic Information Spaces**

Classification: Dual-Use / Defence Ready
Status: Research Prototype (TRL 5-6)
Affiliation: Tomorrow University of Applied Sciences / TruthShield, Aachen

---

## What it is

TruthShield is a research prototype investigating whether visible, evidence-based counter-speech can correct distorted norm perceptions in algorithmically mediated public discourse.

The project addresses a structural gap identified across the counter-disinformation landscape: existing systems detect disinformation effectively but fail to translate detection into communicative impact at the point of exposure. TruthShield explores an integrated pipeline that connects detection with scalable, platform-native intervention and long-term resilience building.

## What it is not

TruthShield does not remove content, does not judge opinions, and does not operate as a political actor. The system is designed for proportional, auditable, and transparent intervention under governance constraints. Every generated intervention is declared as AI-assisted and requires human approval before publication.

## Research context

The theoretical foundation draws on empirical research demonstrating that a small fraction of accounts produces a disproportionate share of misleading content, systematically distorting collective norm perceptions in digital spaces. The project investigates whether targeted, visible counter-speech at high-impact nodes can function as social proof that restores accurate norm perception and reactivates bystander engagement.

The prototype is subject to ongoing academic evaluation. An Extended Abstract has been submitted to the bidt Digital Transformation Research Conference 2026 (Session: Communication and Society). The system is being validated within a pan-European consortium (DE, AT, BG, UA) under Horizon Europe Cluster 2 (Democracy and Governance).

## Architecture

The pipeline integrates four operational layers:

**Detection** — Hybrid multi-channel data acquisition across academic, commercial, and institutional access points. Prioritization follows a concentration-based logic derived from source distribution research.

**Intervention** — Multiple adaptive persona profiles generate platform-native responses calibrated to topic domain and target audience. The design is grounded in research showing that source credibility and perceived similarity causally influence correction reception.

**Education** — Each intervention simultaneously functions as a micro-inoculation unit, exposing the underlying manipulation technique. Dedicated profile channels provide short-format explainer content for cumulative resilience building over time.

**Evaluation** — Offline benchmarking against a corpus of 1,247 high-frequency claims (health, climate, politics). Mixed-effects regression models estimate predictors of engagement potential across persona types and claim categories.

## Safety and governance

These principles are non-negotiable:

- Human-in-the-loop validation for all interventions (median review time: 87 seconds)
- Full transparency: every intervention is declared as AI-assisted and human-reviewed
- Source-first methodology: traceable evidence chain for every factual claim
- EU AI Act Article 14 compliance (human oversight) with systematic audit logging
- No covert operations, no astroturfing, no undisclosed automation
- Optimization is limited to style and delivery; factual content is never algorithmically altered
